# Enterprise AI Visibility Checklist

Use this checklist to assess your organization’s visibility into AI usage.

---

## 1. Tracking AI Usage

[ ] Can we track AI usage across employees and departments?  
[ ] Do we know which AI tools are being used?  
[ ] Do we understand how frequently these tools are used?  
[ ] Do we know what data is being input into AI tools?  
[ ] Do we know what outputs are being generated and where they go?  

---

## 2. Shadow AI Detection

[ ] Can we detect AI tools being used without approval?  
[ ] Do we have visibility into external AI platforms (e.g. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini)?  
[ ] Can we identify internal workflows built using AI?  
[ ] Are API-based AI integrations visible and monitored?  
[ ] Do we know where sensitive data may be exposed to AI systems?  

---

## 3. AI Usage Audit

[ ] Have we conducted a full audit of AI usage across teams?  
[ ] Do we know which teams are using AI and for what purposes?  
[ ] Do we have an inventory of AI tools and use cases?  
[ ] Have we identified data exposure risks?  
[ ] Do we have clear ownership for AI usage within teams?  

---

## 4. Discovery of AI Tools

[ ] Can we list all AI tools used across the organization?  
[ ] Do we have a process to discover new AI tools as they are adopted?  
[ ] Are decentralized and team-specific tools visible?  
[ ] Do we track internally built AI workflows and applications?  

---

## 5. Governance Readiness

[ ] Are we basing governance decisions on actual usage data?  
[ ] Do we have visibility before enforcing policies?  
[ ] Are controls aligned with real-world AI usage?  
[ ] Do we continuously monitor AI usage over time?  

---

## How to Use This

- If multiple sections have gaps, your organization likely lacks visibility into AI usage  
- Start with tracking and discovery before implementing governance  
- Reassess regularly as AI adoption evolves  

---

## Key Principle

You cannot govern what you cannot see.

Enterprise AI visibility is the foundation for security, compliance, and control.
