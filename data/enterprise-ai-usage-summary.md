# Enterprise AI Usage Summary

This summary is based on analysis of enterprise AI usage across 429 respondents.

It highlights key patterns in how AI is being adopted across organizations.

---

## Key Observations

- AI adoption is widespread across teams and functions  
- Multiple AI tools per employee is common  
- Usage is often driven by individual employees, not centralized teams  
- Tracking of AI usage is minimal or non-existent  
- Governance policies lag behind actual behavior  
- Sensitive data is being shared with AI tools in many cases  

---

## Core Insight

AI adoption is accelerating faster than enterprise visibility.

Organizations are using AI extensively, but lack a clear understanding of:

- Who is using AI  
- What tools are being used  
- What data is being shared  
- Where risks exist  

---

## The Gap

There is a growing gap between:

**AI Usage → and → AI Visibility**

This gap creates:

- Security risks  
- Compliance challenges  
- Lack of control over data  

---

## Implication

Most organizations are attempting to implement governance without first establishing visibility.

This leads to incomplete or ineffective control.

---

## What This Means

To manage AI effectively, organizations must:

- Track AI usage  
- Detect shadow AI  
- Audit real usage  
- Discover all tools  

Only then can governance be applied effectively.
